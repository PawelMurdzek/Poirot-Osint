# Country OSINT — Ethiopia

Africa's second-most populous country, multilingual, recent civil-war (2020-2022 Tigray War) makes it one of the most OSINT-relevant African contexts. Internet shutdowns and information control complicate access.

---

## Languages

- **Amharic (አማርኛ)** — federal working language, Ge'ez script
- **Oromo (Afaan Oromoo)** — largest ethnic-language, Latin script
- **Tigrinya, Somali, Afar, Sidamic** — major regional languages
- **English** — government documents, media for international audience
- Translation: Google Translate covers Amharic moderately; Oromo / Tigrinya less well-served

---

## Search & Social

- **Google.com.et** — accessible, but **frequent internet shutdowns** during political events
- **Facebook** dominant (when accessible)
- **Telegram** — heavy use during conflict for both regime + opposition messaging
- **Twitter / X** — moderate
- **TikTok, Instagram, YouTube** — growing
- **WhatsApp** — widespread
- **Reddit** (`r/Ethiopia`) — diaspora-led

## Tigray / Ethiopian Civil War (2020-2022) OSINT

This conflict generated a major OSINT corpus, despite the Ethiopian government's communications blackouts:

| Resource | Focus |
|:---------|:------|
| **Bellingcat Ethiopia investigations** | Atrocity verification, satellite imagery |
| **Ethiopia Wars Map / EthioWatch** | Conflict mapping |
| **Africa Defense Forum** | Open-source military analysis |
| **EEPA Briefings (Europe External Programme)** | Crisis reports |
| **Amnesty / Human Rights Watch satellite analysis** | Mass-grave verification |
| **CNN Ethiopia investigations** | Massacre verification (e.g., Mahibere Dego) |

> **Internet shutdowns** were strategic during the war — OSINT had to rely heavily on satellite imagery, social-media leaks via diaspora, refugee testimony.

---

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Tigray War atrocities** — Mai Kadra, Axum, Mahibere Dego, Hawzen — sustained verification work
- **Drone OSINT** (Turkish Bayraktar TB2 deliveries) — civilian + military analyst work
- **Ethiopia-Eritrea relationship** — opaque, OSINT-relevant
- **GERD (Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam)** — Nile water dispute with Egypt + Sudan; satellite OSINT
- **Refugee flows** — DRC, Sudan, South Sudan, Eritrea overlap
- **Russian / Chinese investments** — periodic attention

---

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Personal Data Protection Proclamation** — relatively weak
- **Anti-Terrorism Proclamation (2009, amended 2020)** — broad, used against journalists
- **Press freedom heavily restricted** during civil-war years and after
- **Foreign-analyst risk** — visiting analysts working on conflict topics face entry-bans / detentions
- **Internet shutdowns** are an OSINT problem in themselves — plan for periods when in-country sources go silent
