# Country OSINT — Kenya

English-Swahili East African anchor, vibrant social-media + investigative-journalism scene, Nairobi serves as regional analyst hub for East Africa OSINT (Somalia, Ethiopia, Sudan, South Sudan, Uganda, Tanzania).

---

## Languages

- **English** — official, dominant in media + business
- **Swahili (Kiswahili)** — co-official, widely spoken
- **40+ regional languages** — minor online presence
- **Google.co.ke** — dominant
- Translation: standard tools; Swahili reasonably well-covered

---

## Social Networks

- **WhatsApp + Facebook + TikTok + Instagram + YouTube** — mainstream
- **X / Twitter** — politically central ("KOT — Kenyans on Twitter" is a major political force)
- **Telegram** — growing
- **Reddit** (`r/Kenya`) — moderately active
- **Mobile / USSD access** — significant fraction of Kenyans access internet via feature phones

## Why Kenya Matters as a Regional Hub

- **Nairobi is a regional UN, NGO, and journalism hub** — much East-Africa-focused investigative work happens here
- **Somali / Eritrean / Ethiopian / South Sudanese / DRC diaspora** in Kenya provides source-network for those countries' OSINT
- **M-Pesa mobile-money OSINT** — Kenyan financial-flows dataset is vast; M-Pesa is in many regional investigations
- **East-African Federation OSINT** — regional integration tracking

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Defamation** civil + criminal
