# Country OSINT — Nigeria

Nigeria is **Africa's most populous country**, English-language, with rich social-media activity, declining-but-major investigative-journalism scene, and unique relevance for cybercrime ("Yahoo boys"), corruption, and West-Africa OSINT.

---

## Languages

- **English** — official, dominant in media/business
- **Pidgin English** — heavy social-media use
- **Hausa** — northern Nigeria, Arabic + Latin scripts
- **Yoruba** — southwest, Latin script with diacritics
- **Igbo** — southeast
- 500+ regional languages with smaller online presence
- Translation: standard tools; pidgin / Hausa / Yoruba less well-served

---

## Search & Social

- **Google.com.ng** — dominant
- **WhatsApp, Facebook, Instagram, X, TikTok, YouTube** — all heavy
- **Nigeria has high X/Twitter penetration** — politically influential
- **X/Twitter was banned in Nigeria 2021-2022 (7 months)** — case study in censorship circumvention via VPN
- **Nairaland** ([nairaland.com](https://www.nairaland.com/)) — Nigeria's largest community forum, ~3M users, semi-anonymous
- **Telegram** — growing political use
- **Reddit** (`r/Nigeria`) — active diaspora

## Cybercrime / Yahoo Boys OSINT

Nigeria is the centre of the **"Yahoo boys"** advance-fee fraud and BEC (Business Email Compromise) ecosystem. Major OSINT-relevant patterns:
- **419 scam** material is a long-running OSINT corpus
- **BEC / cryptoscam Nigerian groups** — extensively documented by Trend Micro, Crowdstrike, Mandiant
- **EFCC enforcement** — periodically publishes mugshot rosters of arrested fraudsters

> Nigerian cybercrime work intersects with **broader West African ecosystem** — Ghana, Côte d'Ivoire have parallel scenes.

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Boko Haram / ISWAP insurgency (northeast)** — HumAngle + Bellingcat
- **Lake Chad Basin conflict OSINT** — multi-country (Cameroon, Chad, Niger)
- **Niger Delta militancy** — long-running
- **EndSARS protests (2020)** — community OSINT verifying Lekki Toll Gate massacre
- **Pandora / Panama Papers Nigerian elite holdings** — sustained investigations
- **Election misinformation** — 2023 elections, ongoing
- **Dasuki arms / Abacha loot** — long-running corruption-OSINT corpus

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Cybercrime Act (2015)** — broad, can be applied to online speech
- **Defamation** — criminal in some states
- **Press freedom** — moderate; periodic harassment of journalists
- **State surveillance** — limited evidence of Pegasus but other tooling documented
- **Personal Data Protection Act (NDPA, 2023)** — GDPR-aligned
