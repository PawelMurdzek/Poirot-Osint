# Country OSINT — Morocco (المغرب / Maroc)

Multilingual Maghreb country bridging Arab world, francophone Africa, and Spain/Mediterranean. Substantial OSINT relevance for migration (Mediterranean), surveillance (Pegasus deployment), and counter-terror context.

---

## Languages

- **Arabic (Modern Standard + Darija)** — Darija is Moroccan Arabic, distinct enough that MSA texts may not match colloquial social-media
- **Berber / Tamazight** — co-official, Tifinagh script + Latin variants
- **French** — widely used in business, media, government
- **Spanish** — north Morocco
- **English** growing

> Cross-search across Arabic + French is essential. Darija often Latin-transliterated on social media (Arabizi).

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Pegasus deployment** — Morocco was among the heaviest Pegasus operators globally; targeted journalists, activists, foreign politicians (incl. French president Macron)
- **Western Sahara dispute** — long-running, frequently OSINT-touched
- **Mediterranean migration / Spain border (Ceuta, Melilla)** — Bellingcat, Lighthouse Reports
- **Hashish trade** — major OSINT topic (Rif region)
- **Casablanca financial-hub OSINT**
- **Morocco-Algeria tensions**
- **Moroccan football corruption + 2030 World Cup OSINT**

---

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Press freedom heavily restricted** — many journalists imprisoned in recent years
- **Article 222 / 263 / 446 Penal Code** — used against journalists for sexual / extramarital allegations (a recurring tactic)
- **Lese-majesty equivalents** — criticism of monarchy carries criminal risk
- **Pegasus + Moroccan state-surveillance** — extensive Citizen Lab / Forbidden Stories work; threat-modeling matters
- **Foreign-analyst risk** — entry-bans for sensitive-subject journalists
