# Country OSINT — Indonesia

Fourth-largest country by population, world's largest Muslim-majority population. Distinct platform mix dominated by WhatsApp + Instagram + TikTok with strong local platforms (Kaskus, Detik). Bahasa Indonesia is broadly accessible to Malay-language searches as well.

---

## Languages

- **Bahasa Indonesia** — Latin script, single official language
- **Bahasa Daerah** — 700+ regional languages (Javanese, Sundanese, Madurese, Minangkabau, etc.) — significant content in major ones
- **Bahasa Malay** is mutually intelligible with Bahasa Indonesia (~80% overlap) — combined with [[#See Also|Malaysia]] queries often beneficial
- **English** — common in business, urban professionals
- Translation: Google Translate solid; **DeepL** added Indonesian recently

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **PDP Law (2022)** — GDPR-aligned data protection, full enforcement phasing in 2024-2026
- **ITE Law (Electronic Information and Transactions Act)** — heavily used against journalists / activists for online speech; "defamation" provision Article 27(3) is particularly contested
- **Press freedom** mixed — periodic legal pressure on outlets, ITE Law cases
- **Decentralized political-Islam tensions** affect what's safely-publishable
- **State surveillance** — limited evidence of Pegasus but BSSN active
- **Foreign-analyst risk** — generally manageable but ITE Law has been applied to foreigners on Indonesian platforms

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **2024 elections** — Prabowo win + sustained election misinformation OSINT
- **Papua (West Papua) coverage** — sensitive topic; frequent OSINT around protest / military violence
- **Religious conflict / blasphemy cases** — OSINT verifies viral claims
- **Corruption cases (KPK)** — regularly OSINT-relevant
- **2018 Sulawesi earthquake / 2018 Lombok / 2004 Aceh tsunami** — disaster OSINT methodology
