# Country OSINT — Philippines

English-Filipino bilingual SE Asian country, vibrant journalism scene under sustained legal pressure, heavy social-media-mediated political discourse, key to South China Sea OSINT.

---

## Languages

- **English** — co-official, dominant in media, business, government
- **Filipino (Tagalog)** — co-official, widely spoken
- Many regional languages (Cebuano, Hiligaynon, Ilocano, Bikol, Waray, etc.) — major content reaches social-media but mainstream media is mostly EN+Tagalog
- Translation: Google Translate (uneven for Tagalog)

---

## Social Networks

> Philippines has among the **highest social-media usage per capita globally** — averaged 4+ hours/day in pre-2020 surveys.

- **Facebook** — universal; effectively the internet for many Filipinos
- **WhatsApp** — less common than Messenger
- **Facebook Messenger** — dominant messaging
- **Instagram, TikTok, YouTube** — heavy
- **X / Twitter** — politically influential
- **Telegram** — growing
- **Reddit** (`r/Philippines`, `r/PHinvest`) — active

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Duterte drug war OSINT** (2016-2022) — community + journalism documentation of extra-judicial killings
- **Marcos family wealth** — long-running OSINT, massive corpus from 1986 to present
- **Maria Ressa / Rappler legal cases** — case studies in journalist persecution
- **South China Sea / WPS (West Philippine Sea) disputes** — Chinese maritime militia, fishing-fleet tracking, AMTI / Rappler / Naval-OSINT collaboration
- **Election misinformation (2022, 2028)** — Marcos-Duterte campaigns
- **Typhoon / disaster OSINT** — community-OSINT response model

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Cybercrime Prevention Act (2012)** — Section 4(c)(4) online libel weaponised against journalists
- **Anti-Terror Act (2020)** — broad
- **Press freedom under pressure** — Maria Ressa cases are emblematic
- **Data Privacy Act (2012)** — has GDPR-like elements
- **Pegasus** — limited evidence vs. Philippines but cyber-mercenary threat-modeling relevant
