# Country OSINT — Thailand (ประเทศไทย)

Thai-language ecosystem, distinct from neighbouring SE Asia, monarchy + lèse-majesté legal context creates major restrictions on what's safe to publish.

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Lèse-majesté (Article 112)** — speaking about the monarchy carries criminal liability; major OSINT topic itself but high-risk to research
- **2020-2021 democracy protests** — Twitter-organised, OSINT-tracked
- **Royal Thai Army assets, Sino-Thai elite-business overlap** — recurring investigation topic
- **Thai-Cambodian-Lao border smuggling**
- **Burmese refugee monitoring** — post-2021 Myanmar coup
- **Tourist-industry OSINT** — financial flows
- **Russian sanctions evasion** — Thailand as transit
- **Casino / gambling-OSINT** — borderlands with Cambodia and Myanmar

---

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Lèse-majesté (Article 112)** — extreme legal risk for any criticism / depiction of royal family; foreign-citizen prosecutions have happened
- **Computer-Related Crime Act (2007/2017)** — broad censorship powers
- **Defamation** — civil + criminal
- **Foreign-analyst risk** — moderate; visiting analysts working on royal-family / military topics face entry-ban / detention risk
- **State surveillance** — Pegasus documented use against pro-democracy activists
