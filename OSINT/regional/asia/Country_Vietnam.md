# Country OSINT — Vietnam (Việt Nam)

Vietnam has heavy Facebook + Zalo penetration (the latter is unique to VN), a one-party-state press environment, and growing relevance for SE Asia OSINT — especially regarding South China Sea disputes, supply-chain pivots from China, and sanctions evasion.

---

## Languages & Search

- **Vietnamese (Tiếng Việt)** — Latin script with extensive diacritics (six tones marked); searches without diacritics often work but are noisier
- **English** — common in business, urban professionals
- **Google.com.vn** — dominant
- Translation: Google Translate; **DeepL** has growing Vietnamese support

## News & Media

> Vietnam is a one-party state. All licensed media is state-owned or state-aligned. Independent journalism mostly exists in exile or as anonymous online publishing.

### State / state-aligned
- **Vietnam News Agency (VNA / TTXVN)** — official wire
- **Nhân Dân** ([nhandan.vn](https://nhandan.vn/)) — Communist Party daily
- **Vietnam News** — English daily, state-aligned
- **VnExpress** ([vnexpress.net](https://vnexpress.net/)) — most-read online, state-owned
- **Tuổi Trẻ** ([tuoitre.vn](https://tuoitre.vn/)) — Ho Chi Minh communist youth paper, broader content
- **Thanh Niên** ([thanhnien.vn](https://thanhnien.vn/)) — youth, broader content
- **Lao Động** — labour federation

### Independent / exile / blogs

## South China Sea / Maritime OSINT (Vietnam-specific relevance)

Vietnam is central to South China Sea disputes — substantial OSINT focus on:
- **Spratly / Paracel Islands** — naval confrontations, satellite imagery
- **Chinese fishing fleets** — AIS tracking + commercial-imagery analysis
- **Vietnamese Coast Guard / People's Navy** patrols
- **AMTI (Asia Maritime Transparency Initiative)** at CSIS — pre-eminent SCS OSINT analyst

---

## Sanctions / Supply Chain (post-2022)

Vietnam is a significant locus of supply-chain redirection from China. This drives ongoing OSINT around:
- **Sanctions evasion** — Russian-Vietnamese trade, Chinese-Vietnamese transshipment
- **Manufacturing relocation** — Apple, Samsung, Foxconn investments in Vietnamese facilities
- **OFAC designations** — periodic designations of Vietnamese-incorporated front companies

---

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **One-party state** — direct online activism on political topics carries significant criminal risk for Vietnamese citizens
- **Cybersecurity Law (2018)** — broad, vague provisions used against online activists
- **PDP Decree (2023)** — Vietnam's GDPR-equivalent, but enforcement focused inward
- **Anti-state propaganda** offences — Article 117 / 331 criminal code, used aggressively
- **Foreign analysts** can investigate Vietnamese topics from abroad with limited risk; visiting analysts working on sensitive topics face entry/visa risk
- **Vietnamese state cyber capabilities** — APT32 / OceanLotus is documented, primarily targets Vietnamese diaspora and NGOs
