# Country OSINT — Türkiye / Turkey

Turkey bridges Europe and Asia, with its own distinct internet ecosystem, declining press-freedom environment, and strong relevance to Syria, Iraq, Cyprus, Libya, Caucasus, and Russia OSINT.

---

## Languages

- **Turkish (Türkçe)** — Latin alphabet (since 1928), with ç, ğ, ı, İ, ö, ş, ü
- **Kurdish** — Kurmanji (Latin) and Sorani (Arabic-script) — significant in eastern Turkey, partly suppressed but growing online
- **Arabic** — significant in southeastern Turkey + Syrian-refugee context
- Translation: Google Translate solid; **DeepL** added Turkish in recent years; Yandex Translate also viable

---

## Search

- **Google.com.tr** — dominant
- **Yandex.com.tr** — secondary
- **Bing Türkiye** — niche
- **Yaani** — Turkish state-backed search (Turkcell), low usage
- Google sometimes blocks/throttles in Turkey during political events; VPN circumvention common

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Syrian Civil War cross-border (2011-onwards)** — Turkey is the geographic gateway; Bellingcat / Syrian Archive heavily use TR-side OSINT
- **2016 coup attempt** — community OSINT in real time, official narrative cross-referencing
- **Gülen movement / FETÖ** — government OSINT campaigns vs. Gülenist counter-OSINT (both directions, both questionable)
- **Erdogan family wealth investigations** — Bellingcat / OCCRP / Black Sea
- **Refugee monitoring** — significant OSINT around Syrian/Afghan refugee flows
- **Russian sanctions evasion via Turkey (post-2022)** — Turkey-Russia trade tracking

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Press freedom severely restricted** — Turkey ranks consistently among the worst globally for journalists imprisoned
- **Article 299 (insulting the President)** — criminal offence, used aggressively
- **Article 301 (insulting the Turkish nation)** — used periodically
- **Anti-Terror Law (TMK)** — broadly applied to journalism / academic work touching Kurdish or Gülenist topics
- **Internet law (5651)** — gives BTK broad takedown powers
- **Pegasus + state surveillance** — documented deployment against journalists, lawyers, opposition
- **Foreign analysts** working on Turkish-political subjects can face entry bans / detention; risk-assess travel
