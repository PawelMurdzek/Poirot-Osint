# Country OSINT — Mexico (México)

Mexico has uneven public-records access, dramatic narco-OSINT context, and a vibrant though pressured investigative-journalism scene. Spanish-language ecosystem with deep US-Mexico cross-border entanglement.

---

## Languages & Search

- **Spanish (Español)** — dominant, Mexican variant has distinct vocabulary
- **Indigenous languages** (Nahuatl, Maya, Mixteco, etc.) — minor online presence, mostly oral
- Translation: Google Translate, DeepL
- **Google.com.mx** dominant

## Government & Corporate Records

| Source | Purpose |
|:-------|:--------|
| **RUC (Registro Único de Comercio) / SAT** | Tax-authority registration |
| **PSM (Public Mercantile Registry)** | Corporate registry — state-level, fragmented |
| **CompraNet** | Government procurement |
| **CNBV** ([cnbv.gob.mx](https://www.cnbv.gob.mx/)) | Banking + securities regulator |
| **BMV (Bolsa Mexicana de Valores)** | Stock exchange |
| **DOF (Diario Oficial de la Federación)** ([dof.gob.mx](https://www.dof.gob.mx/)) | Federal Official Gazette |
| **INE** ([ine.mx](https://www.ine.mx/)) | National Electoral Institute — election data, candidate filings |
| **INAI** ([home.inai.org.mx](https://home.inai.org.mx/)) | Transparency / FOIA equivalent — strong infrastructure |
| **Plataforma Nacional de Transparencia** | National FOIA portal |
| **Court records** | **Supreme Court (SCJN)** rulings public; lower-court access patchy by state |
| **Fiscalía (state-level)** | Criminal-justice records — extremely uneven |
| **CURP (personal ID)** | NOT publicly searchable; widely leaked over the years |
| **RFC (taxpayer ID)** | Limited public lookup |

> Mexico's **INAI / FOIA system** is among the most active in Latin America when functional, though under political pressure — INAI faces ongoing institutional threats.

## Narco / Crime OSINT

> Mexico hosts intense narco-OSINT activity. Methodology blends conventional OSINT with social-media monitoring of cartel propaganda.

| Source | Focus |
|:-------|:------|
| **Borderland Beat** ([borderlandbeat.com](https://www.borderlandbeat.com/)) | English-language narco-conflict tracking |
| **Blog del Narco** | Spanish-language narco-content (controversial) |
| **InSight Crime** | Academic / journalistic |
| **Stratfor / private TI** | Commercial reports |
| **CARDON Risk** — paid Mexico-focused threat intel |
| **DEA, CNS, FBI public reports** | US LE perspective |
| **Cartel Twitter / Telegram channels** | Direct-source content (often disturbing; legal risk) |

> Cartel propaganda on social media — narco-corridos on YouTube, Telegram channels, X/Twitter accounts — is significant OSINT material but ethically + legally fraught (some content is criminal to possess in some jurisdictions).

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **43 Ayotzinapa students disappearance (2014)** — sustained OSINT verifying official narratives, GIEI report
- **El Chapo / Sinaloa Cartel** — massive corpus of US court documents (PACER)
- **Cartel-political nexus** — Quinto Elemento, Animal Político
- **Migration / border OSINT** — US-Mexico border crossing tracking
- **Pegasus** — confirmed Mexican-government use against journalists, activists; documented by Citizen Lab + R3D

---

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Mexico is among the deadliest countries for journalists globally** — serious threat environment for in-country investigators
- **Narco-cartel intimidation** is real for OSINT analysts; sock-puppet OPSEC is not theoretical
- **GDPR-like LFPDPPP** — federal data-protection law
- **Security risk**: foreign analysts visiting Mexico for OSINT field work require careful planning
- **Pegasus** documented heavy use against civil society — threat-modeling matters
