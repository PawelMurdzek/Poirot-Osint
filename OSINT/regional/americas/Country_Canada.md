# Country OSINT — Canada

Canada's OSINT environment overlaps significantly with the [[Country_USA|United States]] but has its own distinct corporate registries (federal + provincial), French-language Quebec ecosystem, and stronger privacy regime (PIPEDA + provincial laws).

---

- **English** — dominant
- **French (Québécois)** — official, dominant in Quebec
- Indigenous languages — minor online presence

## Government & Corporate Records

| Source | Purpose |
|:-------|:--------|
| **Corporations Canada** ([corporationscanada.ic.gc.ca](https://www.ic.gc.ca/eic/site/cd-dgc.nsf/eng/home)) | Federal corporate registry — free search, paid filings |
| **Provincial corporate registries** | Each province (Ontario MOG, Quebec REQ, BC Corporate Online, Alberta Corporate Registry, etc.) — varying transparency |
| **Quebec REQ** ([registreentreprises.gouv.qc.ca](https://www.registreentreprises.gouv.qc.ca/)) | Strongest provincial registry — directors, shareholders (limited), addresses |
| **Beneficial Ownership (federal)** | Federal BO register added 2024, public-search subset |
| **Government of Canada Open Data** ([open.canada.ca](https://open.canada.ca/)) | National open-data portal |
| **CanLII** ([canlii.org](https://www.canlii.org/)) | Free case-law database for federal + provincial courts |
| **CSC (Canadian Securities Commissions)** — coordinated via SEDAR+ | [sedarplus.ca](https://www.sedarplus.ca/) — listed-company filings |
| **TSX, TSXV, CSE** | Stock exchanges |
| **Office of the Superintendent of Bankruptcy** | Bankruptcy records — paid lookup |
| **Land titles** | Provincial — Ontario, BC have OnLand / Land Title and Survey Authority; AB, SK, QC each own systems |
| **Court records** | Federal Court online; provincial varies (Ontario CAS, BC Civil Resolution Tribunal, etc.) |
| **Elections Canada** | Campaign finance, candidate filings |
| **Office of the Commissioner of Lobbying** | Federal lobbying registry |
| **Access to Information (federal FOIA)** | [open.canada.ca/atip](https://open.canada.ca/en/access-information-privacy) |

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **PIPEDA** (federal) + provincial privacy acts (especially Quebec Law 25, BC PIPA, Alberta PIPA) — generally GDPR-aligned, sometimes stricter
- **Quebec Law 25** — particularly strict, parallels GDPR closely with extra requirements
- **Defamation** — civil liability, claimant-friendly; Quebec defamation under Civil Code differs from common-law provinces
- **Press shield** — Journalistic Sources Protection Act 2017
- **CSE (signals intelligence)** — Canada's Five Eyes intelligence partner; OSINT-relevant primarily as adversary capability
