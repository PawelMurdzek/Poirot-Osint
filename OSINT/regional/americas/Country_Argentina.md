# Country OSINT — Argentina

Argentina has notably-strong investigative-journalism culture, decent public-records access, recurring economic-crisis OSINT relevance (capital flight, dollarisation), and significant Italian/Spanish/German diaspora histories.

---

## Languages & Search

- **Spanish (Español)** — Argentine variant ("Castellano Rioplatense"), distinctive vocabulary and "vos" form
- Translation: standard ES tools (Google Translate, DeepL, Reverso)
- **Google.com.ar** dominant

---

## Social Networks

- **WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, X, YouTube** — same baseline as the rest of Spanish-speaking LatAm
- **Mercado Libre** — biggest LatAm marketplace, Argentine-origin
- **Taringa!** — declining, was one of the largest Spanish-language community sites
- **Telegram** — growing political use

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Dollar-blue / capital-flight tracking** — Argentine capital controls drive ongoing OSINT around informal exchange, dolar MEP/CCL pricing
- **Corruption investigations** — long history (Kirchner cuadernos, Macri offshore, Maradona estate)
- **AMIA / Iran-Argentina relations** — sustained investigative focus on 1994 bombing + post-bombing political-corruption ramifications
- **Argentine journalist Daniel Santoro** (Clarín) — Lava Jato cross-border work
- **Vatican OSINT** — given Argentine Pope, ongoing relevance
- **Falklands / Malvinas** — recurring sovereignty OSINT context

---

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Personal Data Protection Law (PDPA)** — pre-GDPR but evolving
- **Press freedom** is generally strong; defamation can be criminal
- **Pegasus / state surveillance** — limited evidence vs. neighbours but watch
- **Economic-crisis context** affects what's "publicly searchable" — paywalls and FOI delays are exacerbated under austerity
