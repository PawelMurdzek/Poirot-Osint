# Country OSINT — Australia

Australia has strong public-records access for corporate and court matters, vibrant investigative-journalism scene, and unique relevance to Pacific-region OSINT (PNG, Solomon Islands, Pacific island states).

## Pacific OSINT (Australia bridges)

Australia is the major Western analyst hub for Pacific-island OSINT:
- **PNG** (Papua New Guinea) — corporate/political contested space
- **Solomon Islands, Fiji, Vanuatu, Samoa, Tonga**
- **China-Pacific competition** — major analyst topic since 2022 Solomons-China security pact
- **Lowy Institute** — Australian think tank, Pacific OSINT analysis
- **ABC Pacific** — sustained Pacific journalism

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Privacy Act 1988** — pre-GDPR but strengthening; reform expected to align further with GDPR
- **Defamation law** — claimant-friendly historically; reformed 2021 to add "serious harm" threshold
- **Press freedom** — strong but under pressure (raids on journalists in 2019; metadata-retention laws)
- **National Security Legislation Amendment** — gives ASIO / AFP broad powers
- **Pegasus** — limited evidence of Australian-government use; Five Eyes context relevant
- **Foreign Interference laws (2018)** — restrict undisclosed foreign-government work; risk-aware for analysts in this space
