# Country OSINT — Ukraine (Україна)

Ukraine is **the most OSINT-active conflict zone in history**. Since 2022, every aspect of the Russian invasion has been documented in real time by community OSINT, with methodologies that have become standard for conflict-zone investigation worldwide.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Ukraine OSINT exists in a wartime context. Information that helps the public understand events can also help the adversary target Ukrainian civilians or infrastructure. **Don't publish geolocations of operational military positions, civilian shelters, or live troop movements.** Standard practice is delaying-by-24-48-hours or generalising location.

## Languages

- **Ukrainian (Українська)** — official, growing dominant since 2014 and especially post-2022
- **Russian (Русский)** — historically widespread, particularly in eastern/southern Ukraine; declining as a public-discourse language post-invasion
- **Surzhyk** — Ukrainian-Russian mixed colloquial speech
- Cyrillic script with Ukrainian-specific letters: і, ї, є, ґ
- Cross-search both languages — major content gap if you only search one

## Conflict / War-Specific OSINT (post-Feb 2022)

> The Russia-Ukraine war is the most-documented conflict in history. Major analyst projects and methodologies:

| Resource | Focus |
|:---------|:------|
| **Bellingcat** | Russian war-crime documentation, equipment-loss tracking, propaganda debunking |
| **GeoConfirmed** ([geoconfirmed.org](https://geoconfirmed.org/)) | Volunteer-verified geolocations of conflict footage |
| **DeepState** ([deepstatemap.live](https://deepstatemap.live/)) | Daily-updated Ukrainian-volunteer frontline map |
| **Liveuamap** | Real-time conflict map |
| **ISW (Institute for the Study of War)** | Daily campaign assessments |
| **Oryx** ([oryxspioenkop.com](https://www.oryxspioenkop.com/)) | Visually-confirmed equipment losses (Russian/Ukrainian) |
| **Ukraine Weapons Tracker** | Captured/destroyed equipment, geolocations |
| **Bellingcat Justice and Accountability Unit** | War-crime evidence preservation |
| **Open Source Centre (OSC)** | Russian sanctions evasion |
| **Belarusian Hajun Project** | Tracks Russian movements via Belarusian territory ([[Country_Belarus]]) |
| **Conflict Intelligence Team (CIT)** | Russian milblogger / OSINT analyst collective (in exile) |
| **MediazonaUA / Mediazona Russia** | Russian/Ukrainian casualty tracking via obituaries |
| **HUR (Ukrainian military intelligence)** | Sometimes publishes leaked Russian data; treat as wartime info ops |

### Key methodologies developed / refined in this conflict
- **Equipment-loss verification by serial number** — every photographed vehicle is cross-referenced with manufacturing records
- **Telegram channel forensic** — Russian milblogger content scraped/translated/cross-referenced for unit movements
- **Russian obituary scraping** — Mediazona's casualty-counting methodology pulls regional Russian media obituaries
- **War-crime documentation** — Bellingcat / Truth Hounds / Mnemonic preserve evidence for ICC referral

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **Wartime law** (martial law since Feb 2022) restricts publication of military information. Ukrainian Telegram channels routinely take down geolocated military content.
- **CSO sanctions data** — Ukraine's NACP maintains its own "war sponsors" list of foreign companies still operating in Russia.
- **GDPR equivalent (DPA Ukraine)** applies; investigations on Ukrainian individuals subject to data-protection rules.
- **Ukrainian intelligence services** (SBU, HUR) actively conduct OSINT and counter-OSINT. Foreign analysts publishing in this space are visible targets to Russian counter-intelligence.
- **Russian-language Ukrainian content** complicates analysis — speakers' political alignment is not always evident from language alone.
