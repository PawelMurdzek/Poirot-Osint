# Country OSINT — Belarus (Беларусь)

Belarus shares an internet stack with [[Regional_RUNet|Russia]] (VK, OK, Yandex, Telegram all heavily used) but has its **own** distinct media, registries, and political surveillance context — particularly post-2020 Lukashenko regime crackdown.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Following the 2020 protests, Belarus experienced a mass exodus of independent journalists, with most credible outlets now operating from exile (Poland, Lithuania, Ukraine pre-2022). The regime has criminalised "extremist" labelling for most independent media, including subscribing to specific Telegram channels.

---

## Languages

- **Belarusian (Беларуская)** — official; minority everyday usage. Two orthographies: Narkamaŭka (official Soviet-era) and Klasyčnaja (pre-Soviet, used by some opposition).
- **Russian (Русский)** — dominant in everyday use, business, government.
- Always run searches in **both** languages — strong divide between Russian-language and Belarusian-language content reflects political alignment.

## Search & Tooling

## Notable OSINT Case Studies

- **2020 election & protests** — Bellingcat / NEXTA / volunteer geolocators verified hundreds of police-violence incidents.
- **Ryanair flight FR4978 hijack (May 2021)** — flight-tracking + ATC audio + corporate filings exposed regime fabrication of bomb threat to arrest journalist Roman Protasevich.
- **2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine via Belarus** — open-source military monitoring (Belarusian Hajun Project, KyivIndependent OSINT, etc.) tracked Russian movements through Belarusian territory.
- **Cyber Partisans leaks** — multiple major leaks of regime data (police database, MVD personnel, prison records).

## OPSEC Notes

- **Subscription to "extremist" Telegram channels is criminal inside Belarus** — phone forensics on returnees routinely uncovers this. If you may travel to Belarus, do not have those channels on a phone you bring.
- **Tor + Whonix** strongly recommended for any opposition-source contact.
- **Russian-Belarusian intelligence cooperation is tight** — assume material reachable to FSB is reachable to KGB BY.
- **Belarusian Telegram is heavily monitored** — both regime and Cyber Partisans monitor it; sock-puppets have a short half-life.
