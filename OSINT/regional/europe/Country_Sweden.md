# Country OSINT — Sweden (Sverige)

Sweden has **uniquely transparent public records** — the constitutional principle of *offentlighetsprincipen* (Principle of Public Access) makes most government documents directly accessible. Personal data (income, property, vehicle ownership) that's private elsewhere in Europe is publicly searchable here.

## Government & Corporate Records

> Sweden's public-records access is **exceptional** — many things gated elsewhere are free here.

| Source | Purpose |
|:-------|:--------|
| **Bolagsverket** ([bolagsverket.se](https://www.bolagsverket.se/)) | Companies registry — basic search free, full filings paid |
| **Allabolag.se** ([allabolag.se](https://www.allabolag.se/)) | Free corporate aggregator with extensive history |
| **Hitta.se** / **Eniro.se** / **Ratsit.se** | **Free people-search** — Swedish addresses, phone, age, sometimes income/property |
| **Skatteverket** (Tax Agency) | Income data is **public** — anyone's prior-year income is requestable |
| **Bilregistret** (Vehicle registry) | Vehicle owners — partly public |
| **Lantmäteriet** | Land registry; basic data partly free |
| **Kronofogden** | Enforcement Authority — public debt-collection records |
| **Court records** | All court documents are public records (with limited exceptions for sealed cases) |
| **Riksbanken** | Central bank |
| **FI (Finansinspektionen)** | Financial supervisory authority |
| **NASDAQ Stockholm** | Stock exchange |
| **Riksdag** | Parliamentary records |
| **Open Data Sweden** ([oppnadata.se](https://www.oppnadata.se/)) | Open-data portal |

> A Swedish person's name plus city often returns: home address, phone, age, prior taxable income, registered vehicles, current/past company directorships. **This is normal and legal in Sweden.**

## Notable OSINT Patterns

- **Russian sanctions evasion via Swedish corporate vehicles** — periodic exposés
- **Wagner / Russian-naval Baltic activity** — extensively tracked
- **Gang violence (2020s wave)** — community OSINT around shootings, often via Flashback discussion + court records
- **Nord Stream 2 + Bornholm pipeline incident (2022)** — open-source naval/seismic OSINT
- **Tax-records publication** is itself an OSINT phenomenon — Swedish journalism routinely uses individual income data

## OPSEC / Legal Notes

- **GDPR + Swedish data-protection law** apply, but **carve-outs for journalism/research** are strong
- **Personal data is more public by default** than elsewhere — but processing it at scale still triggers GDPR
- **Press freedom** is constitutionally protected (1766 Freedom of the Press Act, the world's oldest such law)
- **Source protection** — meddelarfrihet — constitutionally enshrined; among the world's strongest
- **State surveillance (FRA)** — signals intelligence; OSINT-adversary capability
